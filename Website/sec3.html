Common online privacy threats and how to counteract them

There are a number of different ways in which your privacy can be affected online, this can include;

  1. Backdoor

    These are bugs or exploits used to bypass security controls and gain access to your device or computer often from outdated or unpatched software. The solution to this, although hard to detect - keep devices up-to-date with the latest patches.

  2. Direct-Access Attacks

    An attacker gains physical access to your device or computer. The solution to this is, protect your devices in locked rooms/houses/offices when unattended and password protect them.

  3. Eavesdropping

    An attacker gains access to a network in order to monitor it and gain information. The solution to this is, keep up-to-date antivirus, anti-malware, firewall and possibly download a network monitor to view your network traffic for unusual activity.

  4. Malware

    A program or software with malicious code, designed to infiltrate or damage a user’s computer or network.
    Malware can come in many different forms:
    Viruses – Malicious programs disguised or hidden within other programs that can replicate themselves, they can damage or modify data.
    Worms – Similar to viruses but often spread through email.
    Trojans – Malicious programs disguised as legitimate programs, cannot spread by themselves instead trick users into executing the program.
    Spyware – Malicious programs that monitor user activity on a host or network such as personal information and banking details and return the information to the attacker.
    Ransomware – Malicious programs that attempt to extort money from the victim, different levels of ransomware with some being ineffective and some encrypting files and documents until ransom is paid.

    The solutions to this are:
    Keep up-to-date antivirus and anti-malware.
    Use caution when downloading or opening files/links/attachments.
    Do not click pop-ups.
    Only use freeware from legitimate sources.
    Be careful with website URLs.

  5. Social Engineering

    Social engineering is a term referring to the manipulation or tricking of someone into giving out personal details.
    These three different methods of social engineering attacks are generally used together but can work independently.
    Blagging – An attacker will create an imaginary scenario to fool a victim into disclosing personal details. E.g. Nigerian prince fraud/an attacker claiming to be your bank or position of authority.
    Phishing – An attacker will send emails and texts to their victims attempting to manipulate personal details generally through links. E.g. A fake lottery or prize draw/impersonating a bank.
    Pharming – An attacker will create domain names very similar to popular websites and clone them, they will also send out fake links that will re-direct to their domain fooling people into entering their details. E.g. Ebayy.com domain name which looks identical to the eBay website.

    Solution
    Be vigilant online when releasing personal details.
    Use caution when downloading or opening files/links/attachments.
    Be careful with website URLs.
    Be vigilant if using a public computer.
    Do not be fooled by something online that seems too good to be true.

  6. Misconfigured Access Rights

    This is where incorrect permissions are set up allowing unauthorised individuals to view data or information not authorised to them. The solution for this is, setting up the correct access rights and permissions on a computer or network when used by many different users is essential for data privacy and integrity.

  7. Default and Weak Passwords

    A very common way for an attacker to gain access to computer when using default and weak passwords.

    Solutions
    There are many sites and tools available to create a random selection of characters for you, but a good password should follow the general guideline;
    A mix of upper and lower case characters
    Special characters/symbols
    Numbers
    Contain at least 8 characters
    Not something that is easily guessed by knowing you
